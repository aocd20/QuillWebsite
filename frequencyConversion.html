Frequency Conversion

Changing the colour of light at the quantum level

From distributed quantum computing to secure communications, many future quantum technologies are inherently dsitributed, requiring physically separated devices to interface coherently with one another. However, many of these component technologies, such as single photon sources or quantum memories, will only emit or interact with light at specific wavelengths, while the optical fibres used to link them transmit best at others. Our fibres support a nonlinear optical process called Bragg-scattering four-wave mixing, which can be used to selectively create and annihilate photons from specific optical modes.
